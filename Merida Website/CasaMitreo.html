The House of Mithraeum is one of the best examples of a Roman domus (stately home) still standing today. It was built at the end of the first century BC and abandoned at the end of the fourth century.

It begins with an entrance hall, an Atrium for offerings and a Tablinum where the dominus managed his business. There are also several rooms, a cistern and two courtyards, one of them with a pond. Numerous mosaics have also been preserved with big success.
