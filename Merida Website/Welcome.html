Mérida was founded as a Roman colony in 25 BC by order of Emperor Octavio Augusto to serve as a place of retreat for veteran soldiers (emeritos), hence its original Roman name "Emerita Augusta".

Considered then one of the most important cities in Hispania, it was provided with all the comforts of a large Roman city and served as the capital of the Roman province of Lusitania.

Mérida passed into Muslim hands in 713 AD and was renamed Mārida Andalusí. It continued to be the most important population centre in western Al-Andalus.

The city returned to Christian hands in 1230 during the decline of the Muslim reign in Al-Andalus.

In 1983 it was designated capital of the autonomous community of Extremadura in recognition of its past splendour.

Merida has one of the largest archaeological heritages (mainly Roman) in Spain. In recognition of this heritage, the city of Rome sent to Merida in 1997 a reproduction of its famous statue, the "Capitoline Wolf".
